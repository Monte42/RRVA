    President's Letter: August 2, 2019

    Dear Homeowner:

       Hope you are having a good summer.

       In June, we asked homeowners who were interested in serving on our Board of Directors to submit their resumes for inclusion on the ballot for the election of directors at our annual meeting this Columbus Day weekend. As I indicated, we will be electing three persons for a two-year term to our Board of Directors. No one has submitted their resume. The three incumbent directors - Greg Gomez, Jerald Stein and myself have all agreed to stand for re-election.

       We are continuing to assess what additional work will be done in connection with our water system and hope to gain some clarity in the next few weeks. We will keep you informed of any decisions.

       Please note that the charge for the annual trash removal will be reflected on your September statement.

    Enjoy the rest of the summer!

    President's Letter: September 23, 2019

    Dear Homeowner:

       As you know, the 2019 Annual Meeting of Homeowners will be held at 10 a.m. on Saturday, October 12th in the Clubhouse and we urge you to attend this meeting. However, if you are unable to attend and have not as yet submitted your proxy, please submit your proxy in person, by fax (607-326-2927), by pdf (rrvainc@catskill.net), or by mail. Your proxy was sent to you on September 10th. If you need another copy, please contact Wendy.

       Please also note that registration of those homeowners who attend the Annual Meeting will take place at the entrance to the Clubhouse beginning at 9:30 a.m. If you attend and have not as yet submitted a proxy, you will be provided with ballots for the two matters on the agenda. In addition to the matters on the agenda, there will be reports on our financial condition, status of work by NYSEG and Denver Water and as usual, a Q&A period. Please plan to arrive early so that we can start the meeting promptly at 10 a.m.

       You will undoubtedly note that the monthly statement for October includes a charge for school taxes relating to our common grounds and for garbage collection fees. Because of certain legal complications relating to our garbage collection firm we were unable to determine the fees until recently.

       On November 5, 2019 there will be a number of important local elections held in Roxbury, including for Town Supervisor, Council members, Tax Collector, Town Clerk, etc. We urge you to consider voting here, where each vote is meaningful. Please visit the Delaware County Board of Elections website at www.co.delaware.ny.us for information about registering and obtaining an absentee ballot.

    Winter is fast approaching and the following items should be addressed:
       If your chimney was not cleaned or inspected last year and was used with any consistency, please call your chimney sweep and arrange to have it done this year. If you want RRVA to provide your contractor with a key, please notify us 24 hours in advance, Monday through Friday.
       Crawl spaces must be closed for the winter. If you choose not to do this yourself, please notify the office no later than October 31, 2019 and this task will be done for you at a charge of $30.00. Winter House Watch, if you choose to sign up for this valuable service, please submit the accompanying form and make the appropriate payment of $40.00 per month. Those of you not on “Winter House Watch” service that request occasional inspections will be billed $50.00 per inspection. Any emergency services provided by a RRVA employee will be billed at $50.00 per hour plus materials.
       With the consistent harsh winters of the last few years, a number of homes have suffered frozen pipes. Please consider this fact when determining whether to sign up for our winter house watch service.

       Please remember that it is your obligation to keep your decks clear of leaves and snow. If left on the decks, leaves, moss and snow may cause rot on the siding and deck boards which increases our and your maintenance costs.
       Below are the names and phone numbers of chimney sweeps and firewood providers who have provided services for RRVA homeowners in recent years. We are providing their names for your convenience, but are not endorsing any of them. Other providers are listed in local newspapers (Catskill Mtn News, Mountain Eagle) and telephone directories.

    President's Letter: October 25, 2019

    Dear Homeowner:

       It was a pleasure to see many of you at our annual meeting held on October 12th. In the event you did not attend, I am pleased to inform you that three incumbent directors, Greg Gomez, Jerry Stein and myself were re-elected to two-year terms. Continuing as directors until the 2020 Annual Homeowners’ Meeting are Chris Cook, Dorothy Flynn, Bruce Mishkin and Joan Roberts.

       Proposal two on the agenda passed overwhelmingly. To recap, the proposal was to approve a special capital assessment of $180.00 per home payable in twelve consecutive $15.00 monthly installments beginning in January 2020.

       We discussed certain planned and suggested capital improvements and announced the Board’s intention to replace the decking at the pool with a composite material by next summer. We also announced that we were working on a new website as part of an initiative to make RRVA better known and to enhance the values of the homes in our community. We sought suggestions from homeowners as to what information/ links/live feeds they would like to see on the website. If you have any suggestions, please submit them to the office.

       At an organizational meeting of the Association's Directors held that same day, the following officers of the Association were elected to serve until the next Annual Homeowners’ Meeting: Bruce Mishkin, Vice- President; Dorothy Flynn, Treasurer; Joan Roberts; Secretary and myself as President. We hope to be able to share with you more concrete information as to the start of work on the next phase of improvements (installation of cut-off valves for each of the clusters and the replacement of the water valves at each of the homes) to our water system later this year. We are also awaiting an update from NYSEG as to when they expect to replace the power line to the lower clusters.

       An issue that was raised at the meeting, was the concern evidenced by many homeowners about cars speeding down our roads. We have identified speed bumps that can be installed this coming spring if speeding continues. We have no desire to install the speed bumps but will do so if necessary.

       One of the homeowners raised his concern about our rental policy, seeking to permit unlimited rentals during the winter months. We promised to discuss this suggestion and others at our Memorial Day weekend town meeting in 2020.

       A copy of the Association’s snow removal policy and Winter Guidelines is enclosed. It is wise to prepare now for what may be another cold and snowy winter. Also attached is a Winter Watch form, for those of you who wish to utilize this valuable service and have not signed up yet. If applicable, please inform your tenants and guests about our snow removal policy and our Winter Guidelines.

    Wishing you a wonderful Thanksgiving holiday.

    President's Letter: December 21, 2019

    Dear Homeowner:

       During the winter season, please remember to call the office if you are arriving before Friday afternoon so that our staff can clean your walkway if necessary. Your walkway will not be cleared otherwise. Voice mail messages are checked throughout the business day. In event of snow, our roads are plowed before walkways are cleared. It is the Association's policy to clear walkways during the daytime once there is an accumulation of 2” of snow, but when possible, the staff attempts to remove snow sooner. Please do not park in front of walkways. It makes it nearly impossible for the staff to remove the snow and provide residents and emergency vehicles access to the homes. So as not to impede the snow removal process, all objects must be moved away from the walkways and your steps. During snow removal, it's impossible to see and move around these obstacles and they get caught in the snow blower blades, breaking the equipment - it is also extremely dangerous to the staff using the equipment. Please remove lights, mats, pumpkins, garbage cans, rocks, planters, extension cords, etc. by January 1, 2020. If the items are not removed by this date, the staff will do so and place them on your porch. RRVA cannot be responsible for damage to these items. We'd like to thank you in advance for your co-operation. On this month's statement you'll find the first of twelve installment charges of $15.00 for the special capital assessment. Also attached is a copy of the Minutes of the Annual Meeting of October 12, 2019. I also need to draw your attention to two problems that continue to plague us. The first problem relates to dog owners allowing dogs to roam unleashed within our community. The second problem relates to homeowners and guests who either do not pick up dog waste, or bag the waste and either throw it into the woods or leave it at the side of the road or next to a garbage can. The failure to either keep dogs under leash when outside of a townhouse or bag dog waste and remove it appropriately are punishable by a fine. Please consider our snow removal staff, your neighbors and friends and comply with our Associations policies and regulations.

    On behalf of the Directors and Staff of Roxbury Run Village I would like to wish you all a most happy holiday season and new year.

    Steven Glusband, President Bruce Mishkin, Vice-President Dorothy Flynn, Treasurer Joan Roberts, Secretary Greg Gomez, Director Chris Cook, Director Jerry Stein, Director

    Staff - Richard Andersen, Wendy Nief, Terry Haynes and Gary DuMond

    Note: Payments received after Dec 20, 2019 will be reflected on your next statement.
