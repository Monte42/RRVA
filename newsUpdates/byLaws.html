    By-Laws of the RR Village Association, Inc.

    By-Laws of the RR Village Association, Inc.



    ARTICLE I

    The name of the corporation is RR VILLAGE ASSOCIATION, INC. The principal office of the corporation shall be located at Roxbury Run Village, Denver, New York.

    ARTICLE II

    Section 1. Village Association shall mean and refer to RR VILLAGE ASSOCIATION, INC., its successors and assigns.

    Section 2. Property shall mean and refer to that certain real property described in the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions, and such additions thereto as may hereafter be brought within the jurisdiction of the Village Association.

    Section 3. Common Area shall mean all real property owned by the Village Association for the common use and enjoyment of the Owners, together with the roadways and parking areas with respect to which an easement is granted to the Village Association under the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions.

    Section 4. Townhouse Unit shall mean and refer to any plot of land shown upon and recorded subdivision map of the Property upon which a Townhouse Unit shall have been or is intended to be built.

    Section 5. Owner shall mean and refer to the record owner, whether one or more persons or entities, of the fee simple title to any Townhouse Unit which is part of the Property, including contract vendees (excluding those having such interest merely as security for the performance of an obligation) and their heirs, executors, devisees, successors and assigns.

    Section 6. Declaration shall mean and refer to the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions applicable to the Property recorded in the Office of County Clerk, County of Delaware, State of New York.

    Section 7. Member shall mean and refer to those persons entitled to membership as provided in the Declaration.


    ARTICLE III
    MEETING OF MEMBERS

    Section 1. Annual Meetings. The annual meeting of the members shall be held on the Saturday of the Columbus Day weekend of each year, at the hour of ten oíclock, A.M. If the day of the Annual Meeting is postponed, the meeting will be held at the next practical date.

    Section 2. Special Meetings. Special meetings of the Members may be called at any time by the President, or by the Board of Directors, or upon written request of the Members who are entitled to vote one-fourth (1/4) of all of the votes of the Members.

    Section 3. Notice of Meetings. Written notice of each meeting of the Members shall be given by, or at the direction of, the Secretary or person authorized to call the meeting, by mailing a copy of such notice, postage prepaid, at least 15 days before such meeting to each Member entitled to vote thereat, addressed to the Memberís address last appearing on the books of the Village Association or supplied by such Member, to the Village Association, for the purpose of notice. Such notice shall specify the place, day and hour of the meeting, and, in the case of a special meeting, the purpose of the meeting.

    Section 4. Quorum. The presence at the meeting of Members entitled to cast, or of proxies entitled to cast, one-tenth (1/10) of the votes of the Members shall constitute a quorum for any action except otherwise provided in the Articles of Incorporation, the Declaration, or these By-Laws. If, however, such quorum shall not be present or represented at any meeting, the Members entitled to vote thereat shall have power to adjourn the meeting from time to time, without notice other than announcement at the meeting, until a quorum as aforesaid shall be present or be represented.

    Section 5. Proxies. At all meetings of Members, each Member may vote in person or by proxy. All proxies shall be in writing and filed with the Secretary. Every proxy shall be revocable and shall automatically cease upon conveyance by the Member of his Townhouse Unit.

    ARTICLE IV
    BOARD OF DIRECTORS

    Section 1. Number. The affairs of the Village Association shall be managed by a Board of seven (7) Directors. No Director shall be required to be a Member of the Village Association.

    Section 2. Term of Office. The term of office of each Director presently serving as such, whose term of office shall not expire currently with the 1987 Annual Meeting of the Members of the Association, shall be one (1) year. The terms of office of the Directors elected at the 1987 Annual Meeting of the Members of the Association and at any subsequent Annual Meeting of the Association shall be two (2) years.î

    Section 3. Removal. Any Director may be removed from the Board, with or without cause, by a majority vote of the Members of the Village Association. In the event of death, resignation or removal of a Director, his successor shall be selected by the remaining members of the Board and shall serve for the un-expired term of his predecessor.

    Section 4. Compensation. No director shall receive compensation for any service he may render to the Village Association. However, any Director may be reimbursed for his actual expenses incurred in the performance of his duties.

    Section 5. Action Taken Without a Meeting. The Directors shall have the right to take any action in the absence of a meeting, which they could take at a meeting by obtaining the written approval of all of the Directors. Any action so approved shall have the same effect as though taken at a meeting of the Directors.


    ARTICLE V

    NOMINATION AND ELECTION OF DIRECTORS

    Section 1. Nomination. Nomination for election to the Board of Directors shall be made by a Nominating Committee. Nominations may also be made from the floor at the annual meeting. The Nominating Committee shall consist of three (3) members. The Nominating Committee shall consist of a Chairman, who shall be a member of the Board of Directors, and two or more Members of the Village Association. The Nominating Committee shall be appointed by the Board of Directors prior to each annual meeting of the Members, to serve from the close of such annual meeting until the close of the next annual meeting and such appointment shall be announced at each annual meeting. The Nominating Committee shall make as many nominations for election to the Board of Directors as it shall in its discretion determine, but not less than the number of vacancies that are to be filled. Such nominations may be made from among Members or non-Members.

    Section 2. Election. At such election the Members or their proxies may cast, in respect to each vacancy, as many votes as they are entitled to exercise under the provisions of the Declaration. The persons receiving the largest number of votes shall be elected. Cumulative voting is not permitted.

    ARTICLE VI
    MEETINGS OF DIRECTORS

    Section 1. Regular Meetings. Regular meetings of the Board of Directors shall be held monthly without notice, at such place and hour as may be fixed from time to time by resolution of the Board. Should said meeting fall upon a legal holiday, then that meeting shall be held at the same time on the next day which is not a legal holiday.

    Section 2. Special Meetings. Special meetings of the Board of Directors shall be held when called by the President of the Village Association, or by any two Directors, after not less than three (3) days notice to each Director.

    Section 3. Quorum. A majority of the number of Directors shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. Every act or decision done or made by a majority of the Directors present at a duly held meeting at which a quorum is present shall be regarded as the act of the Board.

    ARTICLE VII

    POWERS AND DUTIES OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS

    Section 1. Powers. The Board of Directors shall have the power to:
    (a) Adopt and publish rules and regulations governing the use of Common Area and facilities, and the personal conduct of the members and their guests thereon, and to establish penalties for the infraction thereof;
    (b) Suspend the voting rights of a Member and the right to use of the recreational facilities by a Member, his or her tenants or guests, during any period in which such Member shall be in default or arrears in the payment of any assessment levied by the Village Association;
    (c) Suspend the rights of a Member, his or her tenants or guests to use of the recreational facilities for infraction of the Declaration, these By-laws or any published Rules or Regulations by such Member or his or her tenants or guests. Such suspension, for a period not exceeding ten (10) days, may be imposed by any Director and such suspension may be continued, for a further period not exceeding ninety (90) days, by a majority of a quorum of the Board of Directors held after notice to such Member;
    (d) Levy a fine against a Member for the use of the recreational facilities by such Member, his or her tenants or guests during any period of suspension under Paragraph "(b)" or "(c)" hereof, in an amount up to $50.00 for each violation (each individual person's use of said facilities and each day being deemed separate violations for the purposes hereof), which shall be payable by the Member, and shall be collectible in the same manner as the annual maintenance assessment against his or her Townhouse Unit;
    (e) Exercise or the Village Association all powers, duties and authority vested in or delegated to the Village Association and not reserved to the membership by other provisions of these By-Laws, the Articles of Incorporation, or the Declaration;
    (f) Declare the office of a member of the Board of Directors to be vacant in the event such member shall be absent from three (3)) consecutive regular meetings of the Board of Directors. Employ a manager, and independent contractor, or such other employees as they deem necessary, and to prescribe their duties; and
    (g) $250.00 penalty for any infraction of the Association's architectural codes. This in no way compromises the Association's right to seek redress under its existing mechanisms.

    Section 2. Duties. It shall be the duty of the Board of Directors to:
    (a) cause to be kept a complete record of all its acts and corporate affairs and to present a statement thereof to the Members at the annual meeting of the Members, or at any special meeting when such a statement is requested in writing by one-fourth (1/4) of the Members who are entitled to vote;
    (b) supervise all officers, agents and employees of the Village Association and to see that their duties are properly performed;
    (c) as more fully provided in the Declaration, to:
    (1) fix the amount of the annual maintenance and property tax assessment against each Townhouse Unit at least thirty (30) days in advance if each annual assessment period;
    (2) send written notice of each assessment to every Owner subject thereto at least thirty (30) days in advance of each annual assessment period; and
    (3) foreclose the lien against any property for which assessments are not paid within thirty (30) days after due date or to bring an action at law against the Owner personally obligated to pay the same;
    (d) issue, or to cause an appropriate officer to issue, upon demand by any person, a statement setting forth whether or not any assessment has been paid. A reasonable charge may be made by the Board for the issuance of these statements. If a statement sets forth than an assessment has been paid, such a statement shall be conclusive evidence of each payment.
    (e) procure and maintain adequate liability and hazard insurance on property owned by the Village Association; (f) cause all officers or employees having fiscal responsibilities to be bonded,
    as it may be deemed appropriate; (g) cause the Common Area to be maintained; and
    (h) cause the exterior of the dwellings to be maintained as outlined in Article VI of the Declaration.

    ARTICLE VIII
    OFFICERS AND THEIR DUTIES

    Section 1. Enumeration of Officers. The officers of this Association shall be a President and Vice-President, who shall at all times be members of the Board of Directors, a Secretary, and a Treasurer, and such other officers as the Board may from time to time by resolution create.

    Section 2. Election of Officers. The election of officers shall take place at the first meeting of the Board of Directors following each annual meeting of the Members.

    Section 3. Term. The officers of the Village Association shall be elected annually by the Board and each shall hold office for one (1) year unless he shall sooner resign, or shall be removed, or otherwise disqualified to serve.

    Section 4. Special Appointments. The board may elect such other officers as the affairs of the Village Association may require, each of whom shall hold office for such period, have such authority, and perform such duties as the Board may, from time to time, determine.

    Section 5. Resignation and Removal. Any officer may be removed from office with or without cause by the Board. Any officer may resign at any time giving written notice to the Board, the President or the Secretary. Such resignation shall take effect on the date of receipt of such notice or at any later time specified therein, and unless otherwise specified therein, the acceptance of such resignation shall not be necessary to make it effective.

    Section 6. Vacancies. A vacancy in any office may be filled by appointment by the Board. The officer appointed to such vacancy shall serve for the remainder of the term of the officer he replaces.

    Section 7. Multiple Offices. The offices of Vice-President and of Secretary or Treasurer may be held by the same person. No person shall simultaneously hold more than one of any of the other offices except in the case of special offices created pursuant to Section 4 of this Article.

    Section 8. Duties. The duties of the officers are as follows:

    President

    The President shall preside at all meetings of the Board of Directors; shall see that orders and resolutions of he Board are carried out; shall sign all leases, mortgages, deeds and other written instruments and shall co-sign all checks and promissory notes

    Vice President

    The Vice-President shall act in the place and stead of the President in the event of his absence, inability or refusal to act, and shall exercise and discharge such other duties as may be required of him by the board.

    Secretary

    The Secretary shall record the votes and keep the minutes of all meetings and proceeding of the Board and of the Members; keep the corporate seal of the Village Association and affix it on all papers requiring said seal; serve notice of meetings of the Board and of the Members; keep appropriate current records showing the members of the Village Association together with their addresses, and shall perform such other duties as required by the Board.

    Treasurer

    The Treasurer shall receive and deposit in appropriate bank accounts all monies of the Village Association and shall disburse such funds as directed by resolution of the Board of Directors; shall sign all checks and promissory notes of the Village Association; keep proper books of account; cause an annual audit of the Village Association books to be made by a certified public accountant at the completion of each fiscal year; and shall prepare an annual budget and a statement of income and expenditures to be presented to the membership at its regular meeting, and deliver a copy of each to the Members.

    ARTICLE IX
    COMMITTEES

    The Village Association shall appoint an Architectural Control Committee, as provided in the Declaration, and a Nominating Committee, as provided in the Declaration, and a Nominating Committee, as provided in these By-Laws. In addition, the Board of Directors shall appoint other committees as deemed appropriate in carrying out its purpose.

    ARTICLE X
    BOOKS AND RECORDS

    The books, records and papers of the Village Association shall at all times, during reasonable business hours, be subject to inspection by any Member. The Declaration, the Articles of Incorporation and the By-Laws of the Village Association shall be available for inspection by any Member at the principal office of the Village Association, where copies may be purchased at reasonable cost.

    ARTICLE XI
    ASSESSMENTS

    As more fully provided in the Declaration, each Member is obligated to pay to the Association annual and special assessments which are secured by a continuing lien upon the property against which the assessment is made. Any assessments which are not paid when due shall be delinquent, if the assessment is not paid within thirty (30) days after the due date, the assessment shall bear interest from the date of delinquency at the maximum legal rate of interest, as the same may vary during the continuance of such delinquency, and the Village Association may bring an action at law against the Owner personally obligated to pay the same or foreclose the lien against the Townhouse Unit, and interest, costs, and reasonable attorneyís fees of any such action shall be added to the amount of such assessment. No Owner may waive or otherwise escape liability for the assessments provided for herein by non-use of the Common Area or abandonment of his Townhouse Unit.

    ARTICE XII
    CORPORATE SEAL

    The Association shall have a seal in circular form having within its circumference the words: RR VILLAGE ASSOCIATION, INC. NEW YORK, 1972.

    ARTICLE XIII
    AMENDMENTS

    Section 1. These By-Laws may be amended, at a regular or special meeting of the Members, by a vote of a majority of a quorum of the Members present in person or by proxy.

    Section 2. In the case of any conflict between the Articles of Incorporation and these by-laws, the articles shall control, and in the case of any conflict between the Declaration and these By-Laws, the Declaration shall control.

    ARTICLE XIV
    MISCELLANEOUS

    The fiscal year of the Association shall begin on the first day of January and end on the 31st day of December every year.

    ARTICLE XV
    RULES AND REGULATIONS

    Section 1. All unleashed and/or unrestrained pets are barred from Association property. One complaint, in writing, to the Association stating the date, time, and place of any violation of this regulation, along with a brief description of said animal if possible, will bring a written notice of violation to the cited Homeowner. Any further violation reported, in writing, will incur a fine of $ 50.00 per occurrence, billable and collectible in the same manner as maintenance. It is the obligation of the Homeowner to inform tenants, guests, or other occupants of his or her Townhouse Unit of this regulation, as the fine is assessable against the Owner of the Townhouse Unit where said pet is lodged.

    Section 2. Motorcycles, motor bikes, motor scooters, "mo-peds", trail bikes, dirt bikes, all-terrain vehicles, snowmobiles or any other similar vehicles are barred from Association property; their presence and use being explicitly forbidden in the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions, Article VII, Section 11. One complaint, in writing, to the Association stating the date, time, and place of any violation of this regulation, along with a brief description of said vehicle if possible, will incur a fine of $100.00 per occurrence, billable and collectible in the same manner as maintenance. It is the obligation of the Homeowner to inform tenants, guests, or other occupants of his or her Townhouse Unit of this regulation, as the fine is assessable against the Owner of the Townhouse Unit where said vehicle is kept.

    Section 3. During any period in which any Member of the Association in default or arrears in the payment of any assessment levied by the Village Association, said Member is hereby prohibited from delegating his/her right to the use of the Association's facilities, including the Association's roadways and parking areas, to third parties, including family members, tenants, and/or contract vendees. Any vehicle belonging to said persons found upon the Association's common property under this circumstance will be in violation of this By-law and will incur a penalty in the amount of $50.00 per day per vehicle. This By-law will be enforced by the placement of tire-locking "Boots" upon said vehicles, which shall not be removed until said penalties have been paid by Certified Check, Postal Money Order, or Cash.

    ARTICLE XVI

    The obligation of the Village Association for exterior maintenance of individual Townhouse units, as provided in Article VI of the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions, shall be interpreted as limited to repair and replacement necessitated by normal wear, tear and deterioration over time, and shall not include repair or replacement in case of sudden damage caused by storm, snow, fire, flood, ice, riot or similar events.

    ARTICLE XVII

    The prohibition, in Article VII, section 1, of the Declaration of Covenants, against leaving on any part of the property a structure of temporary character, trailer, unregistered vehicle, tent, shack, garage, barn or other outbuilding, shall be interpreted to include within the prohibition, but not limited to, utility trailers, boat trailers, house trailers, including self-propelled motor homes, whether or not registered, and henceforth no such vehicle shall be left or maintained on roadways, parking areas or any other portion of the property.

    ARTICLE XVIII
    Satellite Dish

    (a) In conformity with the decision of the FCC in CSR 5185-0 (June 16, 1998), one satellite dish antenna may be installed on the chimney surface of any townhouse unit, provided
    (1) the antenna is not more than one meter in diameter, and is one of the following three colors: white, pale beige or pale gray;
    (2) prior to installation the owner files in the Association office
    (A) a certificate of liability insurance, naming RR Village Inc., as an additional insured, in the amount of not less than three million dollars ($3,000,000);
    (B) a written guarantee that the owner shall maintain the said insurance in effect while the antenna is installed, and will indemnify the RR Village Association, Inc., its officers, directors and employees, and hold them harmless, as to any and all liability for personal injury, property damage, or other damages, which may be caused or allegedly caused in whole or in part by the antenna; and
    (C) a written guarantee that whenever requested by the Association the owner will remove the antenna to permit maintenance of the surface by the Association within not more than seven days after receipt of written request to do so.
    (b) Any antenna which is installed or retained in the place without full and continuing compliance with the above provisions will be removed by Association personnel, and the cost of such removal will be added to the owner's maintenance assessment.

    Regulation #2003-1

    Pursuant to authority granted by the Declaration, Article VI and Article VII of the By-Laws, sections 1(c) and 2(h), the Board of Directors of the RR Village Association, Inc. herby adopts the following regulation, to be designated Number 2003-1 of the Association's regulations.

    1. No owner of any Town House Unit shall, in person or through an agent, employee, tenant or guest
    (a) perform any exterior maintenance on his/her unit, including but not limited to painting, staining, carpentry or other repairs or alterations, without first obtaining a permit pursuant to Article V of the Declaration; (b) perform any maintenance or landscaping upon any portion of the owner's lot that lies outside the exterior of his/her Town House Unit, including but not limited to mowing grass; (c) give instructions to Association maintenance, office or other personnel (including personnel under contract with the Association), criticize such personnel to their faces, thi menace any such personnel, or commit any other verbal or non-verbal act with the purpose or effect of harassing or annoying such personnel or making the performance of their duties more difficult or time-consuming. Nothing in this subdivision (c) shall prohibit any owner from communicating requests or complaints to the Board of Directors, in writing; or (d) interfere in any way with Association maintenance, office or other personnel (including personnel under contract with the Association) in the performance of their duties such as snow removal, grounds maintenance, painting and carpentry (e) place or tolerate objects on exterior surfaces of his/her unit, or upon the lawn or any other portion of his/her lot that lies outside the exterior of his/her Town House Unit, or upon common area.

    2. For each and any violation of this regulation the Board may impose a fine of up to $500.00 on the offending owner, and/or impose the Association's cost of rectifying, remedying, replacing or renovating the offending material or article, all to be collected or recovered as provided by Article II, section 3(d) of the Declaration.



    Amendment To Declaration

    OF COVENANTS, CONDITIONS AND RESTRICTIONS
    OF
    ROXBURY RUN VILLAGE

    I
    DEFINITIONS

    Section 1. "Village Association" shall mean and refer to RR VILLAGE ASSOCIATION, INC., a not-for-profit corporation, organized and existing under the Not-For-Profit Corporation Law of New York, and its successors and assigns.

    Section 2. "Property" shall mean and refer to that certain real property described in Schedule "A" annexed to the Declaration recorded in Liber 528 of Deeds at page 653, and such additions thereto as may hereafter be brought within the jurisdiction of the Village Association.

    Section 3. "Common Area" shall mean all real property and improvements thereon owned by the Village Association for the common use and enjoyment of the Owners, excluding, however, Building Sites and/or Townhouse Units acquired by the Association by purchase, foreclosure of lien, or deed in lieu of foreclosure. Said property shall include three types of Common Area:
    Type A - All of the land, other than roadways and parking areas, in each building cluster
    outside of the building lot lines as said building lot lines were established by Roxbury Run Corporation, at or prior to the first conveyance of title by Roxbury Run Corporation to a Townhouse Unit in the cluster;
    Type B - the land in the Village Center excluding the Arts & Crafts Building, more particularly described in Schedule I annexed to this Amendment, as well as all grass, greenbelt and wild areas owned by the Association outside of the building clusters;
    Type C - the roadways and parking areas, including those portions of the roadways and parking areas within or contiguous to individual building clusters.

    Section 4. "Building Site" shall mean and refer to those 251 plots of land shown upon any subdivision map of the property now or hereafter recorded and/or the master plan for the Property, upon which a Townhouse Unit shall have been or is intended to be built. No Townhouse Unit or other structure shall hereafter be erected or constructed upon any Building Site which is unimproved at the date of the adoption of this Amended Declaration, without the affirmative votes of at least seventy-five (75%) percent of the Members, cast in person or by proxy at a meeting duly called for that purpose.

    Section 5. Notwithstanding the provisions of Section 4 of this Article, the Board of Directors of the Village Association shall be empowered to build free-standing structures, such as storage sheds and/or barns, on Association property for the use of the Association.

    Section 6. "Townhouse Unit" shall mean and refer to any Building Site upon which a building lawfully occupied or intended to be occupied for residential purposes, in accordance with this Declaration, shall have been constructed.

    Section 7. "Owner" shall mean and refer to the record owner, whether one or more persons or entities, of a fee simple title to any Townhouse Unit which is a part of the Property (excluding such persons or entities having such interest merely as security for the performance of an obligation).
    Section 8. "Member" shall mean and refer to every person or entity who holds membership in the Village Association.

    II
    PROPERTY RIGHTS

    Section 1. Owner's Easements of Enjoyment. Every Owner shall have a right of use and enjoyment of, in and to the Common Area and its facilities, which shall be appurtenant to and shall pass with the title to every Townhouse Unit subject to the following provisions:
    (a) the right of the Village Association to suspend the voting rights and right to use of the recreational facilities or any other part of the Common Area by an Owner for any period during which any assessment against his Townhouse Unit remains unpaid; and for infractions of its published rules and regulations;
    (b) the right of the Village Association to dedicate or transfer all or any part of its interest in the Common Area to any public agency, authority, or utility for such purposes and subject to such conditions as may be agreed to by the Members. No such dedication or transfer shall be effective unless an instrument signed by two-thirds (2/3) of the Members agreeing to such dedication or transfer has been recorded;
    (c) the right of the Village Association to limit the number of occupants of each Townhouse Unit entitled to the use of the recreational facilities or other portions of the Common Area at any time; and
    (d) the right of the Village Association, in accordance with its Certificate of Incorporation and By-Laws, to borrow money for the purpose of improving the Common Area and facilities and in aid thereof to mortgage its interest in the Common Area.

    Section 2. Delegation of Use. Any Owner may delegate, in accordance with the By-laws, his right of use and enjoyment to the Common Area and facilities to the members of his family, his tenants, or contract vendees who reside in the Townhouse Unit, subject, however, to the provisions of Section 1 of this Article II. No tenant or contract vendee or the guests of either shall be entitled to the use of the Common Area and facilities, or any part thereof, unless and until the Owner shall have provided the Village Association with a memorandum stating that his, her or its Townhouse Unit is being occupied by a tenant or contract vendee, and specifying the names and permanent residence addresses of any such tenants or contract vendees and the period of their occupancy of the Townhouse Unit.

    Section 3. Regulation of Use. The Village Association reserves the right to regulate the use of the recreational facilities and other portions of the Common Area through the establishment of rules and regulations. Such rules and regulations may include, but shall not be limited to:
    (a) Limitation of the number of parking spaces per Townhouse Unit within each cluster, which may differ from cluster to cluster, and designation of parking spots for each Townhouse Unit, provided, however, that each Townhouse Unit shall be entitled to at least one parking area within or adjacent to the cluster in which such Townhouse Unit is located;
    (b) Limitation of the hours of use of the recreational facilities or any parts thereof;
    (c) Limitations on use and occupancy of the recreational facilities to promote the healthful, safe, enjoyment of said facilities by all persons entitled to the use thereof;
    (d) Imposition of fines, as provided for in the By-laws or the rules and regulations, for the infraction of any of its published rules and regulations, which may be assessed against the offending party and the Owner of the Townhouse Unit occupied by such offending party, and which shall constitute liens upon such Townhouse Units and be recoverable in the same manner as is provided in Article IV hereof for the nonpayment of assessments; and/or suspension of the use of the recreational facilities or other portions of the Common Area by the offending party and/or all other occupant of the Townhouse Unit occupied by such offending party.

    III
    MEMBERSHIP AND VOTING RIGHTS

    Section 1. Every Owner shall be a Member of the Village Association. Membership shall be appurtenant to and may not be separated from ownership of any Townhouse Unit. The foregoing is not intended to include persons or entities who hold an interest merely as security for the performance of an obligation, nor shall it include the Village Association, notwithstanding its ownership of one or more Townhouse Units.

    Section 2. Members shall be entitled to one vote for each Townhouse Unit owned, except that when any Member owns more than one Townhouse Unit, such Member shall be entitled, in the aggregate, to only one vote.

    Section 3. When more than one person holds an interest in any Townhouse Unit, all such persons shall be Members, but all such Members together shall constitute but a single membership. The vote for such Townhouse Unit shall be exercised as the Owners among themselves determine, but in no event shall more than one vote be cast with respect to any Townhouse Unit.

    IV
    COVENANT FOR MAINTENANCE, SPECIAL
    AND PROPERTY TAX ASSESSMENT

    Section 1. Creation of the Lien and Personal Obligations and Assessments. Each Owner of any Townhouse Unit by acceptance of a deed therefor, whether or not it shall be so expressed in such deed, is deemed to covenant and agree to pay the Village Association:
    (1) annual maintenance assessment;
    (2) property tax assessments for state and local real property taxes and assessments on the Common Area; and
    (3) special assessments for capital improvements and/or maintenance of a capital or reserve fund, such assessments to be established and collected as hereinafter provided. The annual maintenance assessments, property tax assessments (Common Area) and special assessments (if any) of each Owner, together with interest, costs and reasonable attorney's fees shall be a charge on the land and shall be a continuing lien upon the Townhouse Unit against which each such assessment is made. Each such assessment, together with interest and or late charges, costs and reasonable attorney's fees, shall also be the personal obligation of the person who was the Owner of such Townhouse Unit at the time when the assessment fell due.

    Section 2. Purpose of Assessments. The assessment levied by the Village Association shall be
    used exclusively to promote the recreation, health, safety and welfare of the residents in the Property,
    for the improvement and maintenance of the Common Area, for the payment of real property taxes on
    the Common Area and for the maintenance of Townhouse Units situated upon the Property, as
    provided herein.

    Section 3. Maximum Annual Maintenance Assessment.
    (a) The Village Association upon the vote of the Board of Directors and without a vote of the Members, may increase the annual maintenance assessment each year by not more than five (5%) percent of the annual maintenance assessment for the previous year or, alternatively, said assessment may be increased to conform with the rise, if any, of the Consumer Price Index prepared by the Bureau of Labor Statistics of the United States Department of Labor, Washington, D.C., "U. S. City Average, All Urban Consumers, All Items (1982-84=100)", during the twelve months that ended the preceding August 31.
    (b) The annual maintenance assessment may be increased above that provided in the preceding paragraph (a) by the affirmative votes of a majority of the Members, cast in person or by proxy at a meeting duly called for that purpose.

    Section 4. Special Assessments For Capital Improvements. In addition to the annual maintenance assessment authorized above, the Village association may levy, in any assessment year, a special assessment applicable to that year only for the purpose of defraying, in whole or in part, the cost of any construction, reconstruction, repair or replacement of a capital improvement upon the Common Area, including fixtures and personal property related thereto, provided that any such assessment shall be authorized by the affirmative votes of a majority of the Members, cast in person or by proxy at a meeting duly called for that purpose.

    Section 5. Reserve Fund. Any regular or special assessment may include provision for the establishment or maintenance of a reserve fund. Such reserve fund, or portions thereof:
    (a) may be used in the sole discretion of the Board of Directors for the maintenance and repair of the existing structures or common area improvements; and
    (b) may be used for such other purposes as may be authorized by the affirmative votes of a majority of the Members, cast in person or by proxy at a meeting duly called for that purpose.

    Section 6. Notice and Quorum For Any Action Authorized Under Sections 3 or 4 or 5. Written notice of any meeting called for the purpose of taking any action authorized under Section 3(b) or 4 or 5(b) shall be sent to all Members not less than thirty (30) days nor more than sixty (60) days in advance of the meeting. At the first such meeting called, the presence in person or by proxy of a majority of the Members shall constitute a quorum. If the required quorum is not present, another meeting may be called subject to the same notice requirement. No such subsequent meeting shall be held more than sixty (60) days following the preceding meeting.

    Section 7. Rate of Annual Maintenance and Special Assessments. Annual maintenance and special assessments shall be fixed at a uniform rate for all Townhouse units.

    Section 8. Property Tax Assessments. State and local real property taxes and/or other taxes and/or fees assessed on the Common Area will be paid through the Village Association by all the Owners. Each Townhouse Unit will be assessed by the Village Association for an equal pro-rata share of the real property taxes and/or other taxes and/or fees on the Common Area, which shall be paid annually or as otherwise provided by the Board of Directors.

    Section 9. Annual Maintenance Assessment: Due Dates.
    (a) The Board of Directors shall fix the amount of the annual maintenance assessment for each Townhouse Unit at least thirty (30) days in advance of each annual assessment period. Written notice of the annual maintenance assessment shall be sent to every Owner subject thereto. The due dates shall be established by the Board of Directors, and, unless otherwise provided, the Village Association shall collect each month from the Owner of each Townhouse Unit one-twelfth (1/12th) of the annual maintenance assessment for such Townhouse Unit.
    (b) The Village Association shall, upon demand of a Townhouse Unit Owner or a contract purchaser of a Townhouse Unit, and for a reasonable charge, furnish a statement signed by an officer of the Village Association setting forth whether the assessments on a specified Townhouse Unit have been paid. Such statement shall be conclusive evidence of payment of any assessment therein stated to have been paid.

    Section 10. Effect of Nonpayment of Assessments: Remedies of the Village Association. Any assessments which are not paid when due shall be delinquent. If the assessment is not paid within thirty (30) days after the due date, the assessment shall bear interest from the date of delinquency at the then maximum legal rate per annum, or a late charge in such amount as shall have last been fixed and published by the Board of Directors prior to such delinquency and the Village Association may bring an action at law against the Owner personally obligated to pay the same, or foreclose the lien against the Townhouse Unit, and interest, costs, and reasonable attorney's fees of any such action shall be added to the amount of such assessment. Each Owner, by his acceptance of a deed to a Townhouse Unit, hereby expressly vests in the Village Association, or its agents, the right and power to bring all actions against such Owner personally for the collection of such charges as a debt and to enforce the aforesaid lien by all methods available for the enforcement of such liens, including foreclosures by an action brought in the name of the Village Association in a like manner as a mortgage lien on a real property and such Owner hereby expressly grants to the Village Association a power of sale in connection with said lien. The lien provided for in this section shall be in favor of the Village Association and shall be for the benefit of all other Townhouse Unit Owners. The Village Association, acting on behalf of the Townhouse Unit Owners shall have the power to bid in an interest foreclosed at foreclosure sale and to acquire and hold, lease, mortgage and convey the same. Each Owner hereby assigns to the Association the rents and profits of such Owner's Townhouse Unit and grants to the Association the right to enter upon and to take possession of such Townhouse Unit for the purpose of collecting the same, to let the Townhouse Unit and to apply the rents therefrom, after payment of all necessary charges and expenses, on account of the assessment in arrears. This assignment and grant shall continue in effect until all assessment arrears are paid. In any action to foreclose the lien of the Association, the Association shall be entitled to the appointment of a receiver. No Owner may waive or otherwise escape liability for the assessments provided for herein by non- use of the Common Area or abandonment of his Townhouse Unit.

    Section 11. Subordination of The Lien to Mortgages. The lien of the assessments provided for herein shall be subordinate to the lien of any first mortgage on the Townhouse Unit. Sale or transfer of any Townhouse Unit shall not affect the assessment lien. The sale or transfer of any Townhouse Unit pursuant to foreclosure of a first mortgage or any proceeding in lieu thereof, shall extinguish the lien of such assessments as to payments which become due prior to such sale or transfer. No sale or transfer shall relieve such Townhouse Unit from liability for any assessments thereafter becoming due or from the lien thereof.

    Section 12. Exempt Property. All properties dedicated to and accepted by, a local public authority, and the Common Area, shall be exempt from the assessments created herein. However, no land or improvements devoted to dwelling use shall be exempt from said assessments.

    Section 13. Management Agreements. Each Owner of a Townhouse Unit hereby agrees to be bound by the terms and conditions of all management agreements entered into by the Village Association. A copy of all such agreements shall be available to each Owner.

    Section 14. Insurance. The Board of Directors of the Village Association shall be empowered to secure such fire and extended coverage insurance and public liability insurance covering the Common Area and such other insurance coverage as the Board of Directors in their discretion may determine. The cost of such insurance coverage shall be an expense of the Village Association.

    Section 15. Registration Fee. Beginning sixty days after the recording of this Declaration, all new Members shall be required to pay a $250 registration fee to the Village Association within 30 days of becoming a Member. Such registration fee shall be billable and collectible in the same manner as maintenance assessments.

    V
    ARCHITECTURAL CONTROL

    (a) No building, fence, wall or other structure shall be commenced, erected or maintained upon the property, nor shall any exterior addition to or change or alteration therein be made until the plans and specifications showing nature, kind, shape, height, materials, and location of the same shall have been submitted to and approved in writing as to harmony of external design and location in relation to surrounding structures and topography by the Board of Directors of the Village Association, or by an Architectural Control Committee composed of three (3) or more representatives appointed by the Board. In the event said Board, or its designated committee, fails to approve or disapprove such design and location within seventy-five (75) days after said plans and specifications have been submitted to it, approval will not be required and this Article will be deemed to have been fully complied with.

    (b) Approval of any proposed exterior addition or improvement may be conditioned upon the Owner's assumption of the sole responsibility for the repair and maintenance thereof, in which event, the Village Association shall have no obligation or responsibility for the repair and/or maintenance of such addition or improvement under Article VI hereof.

    (c) In the event of any violation of this Article, the Village Association may initiate such actions or proceedings to enjoin such violation and/or to recover any damages resulting therefrom, as the Board of Directors may deem appropriate. In such event, the Village Association shall be entitled to recover from the offending Owner its reasonable attorneys' fees and disbursements incurred in the prosecution of any such action or proceeding, which shall constitute a lien upon such Townhouse Unit, and, in the event of the non-payment thereof, shall be recoverable in the same manner provided in Article IV for non-payment of assessments.

    (d) In the event that any action or proceeding shall be commenced by any Owner against the Village Association, the Board of Directors and/or the Architectural Committee to compel the approval of any proposed construction, alteration or improvement, and such Owner is the unsuccessful party in such action or proceeding, the Village Association shall be entitled to recover from such Owner its reasonable attorneys' fees and disbursements incurred in the defense of such action or proceeding, which shall constitute a lien upon such Townhouse Unit, and, in the event of the non-payment thereof, shall be recoverable in the same manner provided in Article IV for non-payment of assessments.

    VI
    EXTERIOR MAINTENANCE

    Section 1. Maintenance of Individual Townhouse Units. In addition to maintenance of the Common Area, the Village Association shall provide exterior maintenance upon each Townhouse Unit which is subject to assessment hereunder, as follows: repair, replace and care for roofs, exterior building surfaces, trees, shrubs, grass, walks, and other exterior improvements which shall have been duly approved by the Board of Directors or Architectural Committee (unless such approval shall have been conditioned upon the Owner's assumption of responsibility for the maintenance and repair of such improvements). Such exterior maintenance shall not include any maintenance services other than those specified in the prior sentence, and shall exclude, glass surfaces, screens and screen doors, exterior doors, window fixtures and other hardware, foundations or parts thereof, any underground improvements or utility lines or any portions of the structure within the envelope bounded by the interior surface of the exterior siding, or any utility lines outside of the structure. In the event that the need for maintenance or repair is caused through the willful or negligent act (including, but not limited to the attachment of unauthorized structures or appliances to the exterior surfaces of the unit, or the imposition of an excessive weight load on any horizontal surface either by number of occupants permitted thereon or the weight of objects placed thereon) of the Owner, his family, guests, invitees, tenants or contract vendees, and not covered or paid for by insurance on such Townhouse Unit, the cost of such maintenance or repairs shall be added to and become a part of the assessment to which such Townhouse Unit Owner is subject.

    Section 2. Additional Maintenance by Village Association. In the event an Owner of any Townhouse Unit shall fail to maintain the premises and the improvements situated thereon in a manner satisfactory to the Board of Directors, the Village Association, after approval of two-thirds (2/3) vote of the entire Board of Directors, shall have the right, through its agents and employees, to enter upon said property and to repair, maintain, and restore the Townhouse Unit. The cost of such maintenance shall be added to and become part of the assessment to which such Townhouse Unit is subject.

    VII
    USE RESTRICTIONS

    Section 1. Property is hereby restricted to residential structures and ancillary and accessory uses and buildings in connection therewith, including but not limited to community buildings and recreational facilities, maintenance buildings and the facilities for the water, sewage and refuse collection systems and a cable television antenna system. All buildings or structures erected upon the property shall be of new construction and no building or structure shall be moved from other locations to the property. No building or structure of a temporary character, mobile home, trailer, unregistered vehicle, tent, shack, garage, barn or other outbuilding shall be used on any portion of said Property at any time as a residence, either temporarily or permanently, or shall be left or maintained thereon.

    Section 2. Each Townhouse Unit shall be conveyed as a separately designated and legally described freehold estate subject to the terms, conditions and provisions hereof.

    Section 3. Subject to such additional restrictions as may be imposed by the Village Association, no animals, livestock or poultry of any kind shall be raised, bred or kept in any of said Townhouse Units, except that dogs, cats or other household pets may be kept provided that they are not kept, bred or maintained for any commercial purpose and are not permitted outside the confines of the Owner's Townhouse Unit unless leashed or otherwise restrained. No animals shall be permitted within the boundaries of the Village Center at any time, whether or not restrained.

    Section 4. No advertising signs, billboards, unsightly objects, or nuisances shall be erected, placed or permitted to remain on the Property, nor shall the Property be used in any way or for any purpose which may endanger the health or unreasonably disturb the Owner of any Townhouse Unit or any resident thereof. No business or commercial activities of any kind whatever (other than a home office not requiring third-party visitors) shall be conducted in any Townhouse Unit or in any portion of the Property unless authorized by the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the Members, cast in person or by proxy at a meeting duly called for that purpose.

    Section 5. The Board of Directors may establish rules and regulations for the maintenance and storage of equipment, garbage cans, service yards, woodpiles, or storage piles and may require the screening thereof by adequate planting so as to conceal them from view of neighboring Townhouses, paths and roadways. All rubbish, trash or garbage shall be regularly removed from the premises, and shall not be allowed to accumulate thereon. No clotheslines or other devices for hanging or drying of clothes shall be maintained in any place on the Property except in the interior of a Townhouse Unit.

    Section 6. No fences, hedges or walls shall be erected or maintained upon said Property except such as are installed in accordance with the initial construction of the Townhouse Units located thereon or in accordance with Article V hereof. Except for the right of ingress and egress, the Owners of Townhouse Units are hereby prohibited and restricted from using any of said Property outside the exterior building lines, except roadways and parking areas and except with respect to the Common Area (and nature trails, managed forest area, gardens and lawns other than such areas as are owned by Townhouse Unit Owners) as may be allowed in accordance with rules and regulations adopted by the Board of Directors of the Village Association. It is expressly acknowledged and agreed that this provision is for the mutual benefit of all Owners of Townhouse Units, and is necessary for the protection of said Owners.

    Section 7. All fixtures and equipment installed within a Townhouse Unit, commencing at point where the utility lines, pipes, wires, conduits or systems enter the exterior walls of a Townhouse Unit, shall be maintained and kept in repair by the Owner thereof. An Owner shall do no act nor any work that will impair the structural soundness or integrity of another Townhouse Unit or impair any easement or hereditament, nor do any act nor allow any condition to exist which will adversely affect the other Townhouse Units or their Owners.

    Section 8. No exterior television or radio antennas of any sort shall be placed, allowed or maintained upon any portion of the improvements to be located upon the Property, nor upon any structure situated upon the property. Nothing herein provided shall be deemed to bar or prohibit the installation, by the Village Association, of a satellite or other master antenna system for the use of the Members, if authorized by the affirmative votes of a majority of the Members cast in person or by proxy at a meeting duly called for such purpose.

    Section 9. No action shall at any time be taken by the Village Association or its Board of Directors which in any manner would discriminate against any Owner or Owners in favor of the other Owners.

    Section 10. No snowmobiles, motorcycles, motor scooters, trail bikes or any similar vehicle shall be permitted or used on the Property.

    VIII
    PARTY FOUNDATION EASEMENTS

    Section 1. General Rules of Law to Apply. All Foundations which are built as a part of the original construction of the homes upon the Property and placed under the dividing line between the Townhouse Units or which serve as the foundation for two or more Townhouse Units shall constitute a party foundation, and, to the extent not inconsistent with the provisions of this Article, the general rules of law regarding party walls and liability for property damage due to negligence of willful acts or omissions shall apply thereto.

    Section 2. Sharing of Repair and Maintenance. The cost of reasonable repair and maintenance of a party foundation shall be shared by the Owners who make use of the foundation in proportion to such use.

    Section 3. Destruction by Fire or Other Casualty. If a party foundation is destroyed or damaged by fire or other casualty then, to the extent that such damage is not covered by insurance and repaired out of the proceeds of same, any Owner who has used the foundation may restore it, and if the other Owners thereafter make use of the foundation, they shall contribute to the cost of restoration thereof in proportion to their use, without prejudice, however, to the right of any such Owners to call for a larger contribution from the others under any rules of law regarding liability for negligent or willful acts or omissions.

    Section 4. Weatherproofing. Notwithstanding any other provision of this Article, to the extent that such damage is not covered and paid by the insurance provided for herein, an Owner who by his negligent or willful act causes the foundation to be exposed to the elements, shall bear the whole cost of furnishing the necessary protection against such elements.

    Section 5. Right to Contribution Runs With Land. The right of any Owner to contribution from any other Owner under this Article shall be appurtenant to the land and shall pass to such Owner's successors in title.

    Section 6. Arbitration. In the event of any dispute arising concerning a party foundation, or under the provisions of this Article, each party shall choose one arbitrator, and such arbitrators shall choose one additional arbitrator, and the decision shall be by a majority of all the arbitrators. Should any party refuse to appoint an arbitrator within ten days after written request therefor, the Board of Directors of the Village Association shall select an arbitrator for the refusing party.

    IX
    EASEMENTS

    Section 1. Each Townhouse Unit and the Common Area shall be subject to an easement for encroachments created by construction, settling and overhangs, as designed or constructed by the Declarant. A valid easement for said encroachments and for the maintenance of same, so long as the structure stands, shall and does exist. In the event a multi-family structure containing two or more Townhouse Units is partially or totally destroyed, and then rebuilt, the Owners of the Townhouse Units so affected agree that minor encroachments of parts of the adjacent Townhouse Units or Common Areas due to construction shall be permitted and that a valid easement for said encroachment and the maintenance thereof shall exist.

    Section 2. There is hereby created a blanket easement upon, across, over and under all of said Property for ingress, egress, installation, replacing, repairing and maintaining all utilities, including but not limited to water, sewers, gas, telephones and electricity, and a master television antenna system. By virtue of this easement, it shall be expressly permissible for the utility company to install and maintain underground conduits and/or erect on said Property and to affix and maintain electrical and/or telephone wires, circuits and conduits on, above, across and under the roofs and exterior walls of said Townhouses. An easement is further granted to all police, fire protection, ambulance and all similar persons to enter upon the roadways and Common Area in the performance of their duties. Further, an easement is hereby granted to the Village Association, its officers, agents, employees and to any management company selected by the Village Association to enter in or to cross over the Common Area and to enter any Townhouse Unit to inspect and to maintain and repair a Townhouse Unit or the Common Area provided for herein, to prevent damage to any other Townhouse Unit or the Common Area. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in this paragraph, no additional sewers, electrical lines, water lines, or other utilities may be installed on said Property except as approved by the Village Association's Board of Directors. The easements provided for in this Article IX shall in no way affect any other recorded easement on said premises.

    X
    VILLAGE ASSOCIATION EASEMENT

    Section 1. Grant of Easement. Subject to the terms and conditions of the easements granted in Article IX, the Village Association shall have an easement upon, across and over all roadways and parking areas located on the Property.

    Section 2. Rules and Regulations. The Village Association may impose such rules and regulations regarding use of roadways and use and allocation of parking areas by and among the Members of the Village Association, their families, guests, tenants, invitees and business visitors as the Board of Directors of the Village Association may from time to time adopt.

    Section 3. Maintenance of Roadways and Parking Areas. The Village Association shall maintain and keep all roadways and parking areas located on the Property in good repair at its cost and expense.

    XI
    GENERAL PROVISIONS

    Section 1. Enforcement. The Village Association, or any Owner, shall have the right to enforce, by any proceeding at law or in equity, all restrictions, conditions, covenants, reservations, liens and charges now or hereafter imposed by the provisions of the Declaration. Failure by the Village Association or by any Owner to enforce any covenant or restriction herein contained shall in no event be deemed a waiver of the right to do so thereafter.

    Section 2. Severability. Invalidation of any one of these covenants or restrictions by judgment or court order shall in no wise affect any other provisions which shall remain in full force and effect.

    Section 3. Amendment. The covenants and restrictions of this Declaration shall run with and bind the land, for a term of twenty (20) years from August 10, 1972, after which time they shall be automatically extended for successive periods of ten (10) years. This Declaration may be amended by an instrument signed by not less then seventy-five (75%) percent of the Owners. Any amendment must be recorded.

    Section 4. Annexation of Additional Property. Annexation of additional property shall require the assent of two-thirds (2/3) of the Members, at a meeting duly called for this purpose, written notice of which shall be sent to all Members not less than thirty (30) days nor more than sixty (60) days in advance of the meeting setting forth the purpose of the meeting. The presence of Members or proxies entitled to cast sixty (60%) percent of the votes shall constitute a quorum. If the required quorum is not forthcoming at any meeting, another meeting may be called, subject to the notice requirement set forth above, and the required quorum at such subsequent meeting shall be one- half (1/2) of the required quorum of the preceding meeting. No such subsequent meeting shall be held more than sixty (60) days following the preceding meeting. In the event that two-thirds (2/3) of the Membership are not present in person or by proxy, Members not present may give their written consent to the action taken thereat.

    Section 5. Gender and Grammar. The singular wherever used herein shall be construed to mean the plural when applicable and the necessary grammatical changes required to make the provisions hereof apply either to corporations or individuals, men or women, shall in all cases be assumed as though in each case fully expressed.

    ARTICLE XX – IMPOSE MONETARY FINES

    RESOLVED, The Board of Roxbury Run Village Association, Inc. is hereby empowered to impose monetary fines for any infraction of any rule, regulation, by-law or provision of the Declarations and to establish a written schedule of fines, which fines shall initially range from $50.00 to $500 per infraction, and shall be reviewed and amended by the Board, at least once every two years.



    ARTICLE XIX – Rental Guidelines

    1. No weekend rental of a Townhouse Unit is permitted; only rentals for more than 13 days are allowed.

    2. Every advertisement or other offering for rental of a Townhouse Unit must state, in clearly legible font, “Rentals for less than 2 weeks are prohibited.” Failure to include such notice shall result in a fine to the Owner of not less than $500 per advertisement. Each placement of a violating advertisement shall be considered a separate violation (i.e., placing the same advertisement in 3 different venues would constitute 3 violations). It shall be the renting Owner’s responsibility to inform their agents of this notice requirement. A violation of the notice requirement by an Owner’s agent shall be considered a violation by the Owner.

    3. More than four two week rentals of a Townhouse Unit within any 12 month period shall be considered running a business in violation of Article VII, section 4 of the Amendment to Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restriction of Roxbury Run Village.

    4. This Article shall take effect 60 days after its passage.
